About

Amanda Marie Gentile McEwen is a clinical development and regulatory leader with more than 16 years of experience in small to midsize biotech, centered in the Boston and Cambridge area. She is currently Senior Vice President of Clinical Development at Salubris Biotherapeutics and a program lead. Her work has supported programs across therapeutic areas and modalities, with a focus on moving assets from early development through regulatory approval. Career highlights include contributions to two FDA approved therapies, Inbrija for Parkinson’s disease and Rivfloza for a rare genetic liver disease.

Amanda is also an Executive Fellow at Harvard Business School and is affiliated with Northeastern University’s College of Professional Studies. CPCC brings that same operator mindset to clients, streamline the strategy, then get hands dirty delivering what the team needs next.
