What we do

CPCC works exclusively with early clinical-stage biotech companies to navigate the path from program initiation to the first meaningful clinical inflection point: the data, regulatory decisions, and milestones that unlock the next chapter.

Whether you need a senior thought partner at the investor or FDA table, or someone to build the documents and run the work, CPCC covers the full range. High-level development strategy through hands-on execution and submission-ready deliverables.

Why Now

In today's market, clinical data is the currency of biotech value creation. Partnerships, M&A, and financing rounds increasingly hinge on clean early readouts — not pipeline potential alone. Large pharma, facing patent cliffs and depleted pipelines, is actively hunting for clinical-stage assets with de-risked data packages. In 2022–24, a higher proportion of biopharma deals focused on assets already in clinical development, reversing a prior trend toward earlier-stage innovation. Getting to that first inflection point, efficiently and credibly, is what creates optionality.

About

CPCC is led by Amanda McEwen — LinkedIn — SVP of Clinical Development at Salubris Biotherapeutics, where she leads the JK07 program, a first-in-class ErbB4 agonist in Phase 2b for heart failure.

Amanda has spent more than 16 years inside small to midsize biotech teams in Boston and Cambridge, running programs from first-in-human through FDA-approved therapies, including Inbrija (inhaled levodopa for Parkinson's OFF episodes, approved 2018) and Rivfloza (for primary hyperoxaluria type 1). Her work spans antibody fusions, ADCs, RNAi, inhaled delivery, small molecules, and microbiome therapeutics across neurology, cardiovascular, rare disease, oncology, pain, and GI.

She is an Executive Fellow at Harvard Business School in entrepreneurship and life sciences, and adjunct faculty at Northeastern University in the MS in Regulatory Affairs program.
